üéØ General Objection Handling Principles

  • Never argue or get defensive - If they say "I don't need it," don't say "Yes you do!" Stay calm and respectful
  • Acknowledge their concern first - "I understand," "That makes sense," "Great question" - validates them
  • Answer briefly - Don't give long explanations. Answer in 2-3 sentences max, then redirect to email
  • Always redirect to email - Every objection response should end with asking for their email
  • Stay friendly - Even if they're rude, you stay professional and kind
  • Use "feel, felt, found" technique - "I understand how you feel. Other owners felt the same way. What they found was..."
  • Focus on low pressure - Emphasize "just samples," "no commitment," "review when ready"
  • Create mild urgency - "Competitors are online," "customers are searching now"
  • Know when to let go - If they say no 3 times firmly, thank them and move on professionally
